Savitri Bai Phule


Savitribai Phule was born on 3 January 1831. His father's name was Khandoji Naivese and mother's name was Laxmibai. Savitribai Phule was married to Jyotirao Phule in 1840. Savitribai Phule was the first principal of India's first girls' school and the founder of the first farmer's school. Mahatma Jyotirao is regarded as one of the most important figures in the social reform movement in Maharashtra and India. He is known for his efforts to educate women and Dalits and other backward castes. Jyotirao, later known as Jyotiba, was Savitribai's mentor, mentor and supporter. Savitribai lived her life like a mission, the aim of which was to get widow marriages done, to eradicate untouchability, to liberate women and to educate Dalit women. She was also a poet, she was also known as Adikavyatri of Marathi.


1. Social problems :

When she used to go to school, Brahmins used to throw stones at her. Used to throw dirt on them. 191 years ago, when opening a school for girls was considered a sin, it used to happen. Savitribai is the heroine of the whole country. He worked for every community and religion. When Savitribai used to go to teach the girls, people used to throw dirt, mud, cow dung, excreta on her on the way. Savitribai used to carry a saree in her bag and used to change the soiled saree after reaching school. She inspires very well to keep walking on her path.


2. School setting :

On 5 September 1848, along with her husband, she established a school for women in Pune with nine girl students of different castes. Savitribai and Mahatma Phule succeeded in opening five new schools in one year. The then government also honored him. How difficult it must have been for a female principal to run a girls' school in 1848, it probably cannot be imagined even today. There was a social restriction on the education of girls at that time. Savitribai Phule not only studied herself during that period, but also made arrangements for the education of other girls.


3. Death :

Savitribai Phule died on 10 March 1897 due to plague. During the plague epidemic, Savitribai used to serve the plague patients. Due to serving a child affected by the contagion of plague, he also got contagion. And because of this he died.
