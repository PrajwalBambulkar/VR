Nelson Mandela


Nelson Kholihlahla Mandela (Xhosa: Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela; 18 July 1918 â€“ 5 December 2013) was the first black former President of South Africa. Before becoming president, he was the president of the African National Congress and its armed wing Umkhonto we Sizwe, which opposed centuries of apartheid in South Africa. Due to the anti-apartheid struggle, he spent 27 years in prison on Robben Island where he had to work as a coal miner. In 1990, after an agreement with the white government, he built a new South Africa. He became a symbol of opposition to apartheid in South Africa and around the world. The United Nations decided to celebrate his birthday as Nelson Mandela International Day.


1. Early life :

Mandela was born on 18 July 1918 in Mwezo, Eastern Cape, Union of South Africa, to Gedla Henry Mphakeniswa and his third wife, Nkufi Noskeni. He was the first child of his mother Noskeni and the third of 13 brothers among all the children of his father. Mandela's father, Henry Mwezo, was the tribal chieftain of the town. The chieftain's son was called Mandela in the local language, from which he derived his surname. His father gave him the first name 'Rolihlala', which in translation means " troublemaker". His mother was a Methodist. Mandela completed his early education from the Clarkberry Missionary School. After that he did his schooling from Methodist Missionary School. Mandela's father died when he was 12 years old.


2. Political struggle :

Postage stamp issued on Nelson Mandela in Soviet Union In 1941 Mandela moved to Johannesburg where he met Walter Sisulu and Walter Albertine. Both of them greatly influenced Mandela politically. To make a living, he became a clerk in a law firm, but gradually his activism increased in politics. He stepped into politics to remove discrimination on the basis of color. In 1944, he joined the African National Congress, which had launched a movement against apartheid. In the same year, together with his friends and colleagues, he founded the African National Congress Youth League. In 1947, he was elected secretary of the league. In 1961, Mandela and some of his friends were tried for treason, but they were considered innocent.


On 5 August 1962, he was arrested for inciting workers to strike and leaving the country without permission. He was tried and sentenced to life imprisonment on 12 July 1964. He was sent to Robben Island jail for punishment, but even the punishment did not reduce his enthusiasm. He had started mobilizing the black prisoners in the jail as well. After spending 27 years of his life in prison, he was finally released on 11 February 1990. After his release, he laid the foundation of a democratic and multiracial Africa through a policy of compromise and peace. Apartheid-free elections were held in 1994 in South Africa. The African National Congress obtained 62 percent of the vote and formed its government with a majority. On 10 May 1994, Mandela became the first black President of his country. The new constitution of South Africa was approved by Parliament in May 1996, under which several institutions were established to check political and administrative rights. In 1997, he retired from active politics and two years later, in 1999, he also left the post of Congress-President.


3. Thinking :

Nelson Mandela was a supporter of non-violent path to a great extent like Mahatma Gandhi. He considered Gandhi as a source of inspiration and learned the lesson of non-violence from him.


4. Personal life :

Mandela had three marriages from which he had six children. His family had 17 grandchildren. In October 1944 he married Evelyn Mace, the sister of his friend and colleague Walter Sisulu. Mandela was tried for treason in 1961 but he was found innocent by the court. It was during this trial that he met his second wife, Nomjamo Winnie Medikijala. He married Grace Meikle in 1998, on his 80th birthday.


5. Death :

Mandela died on 5 December 2013 at his home in Houghton, Johannesburg, of a lung infection. He was 95 years old at the time of his death and his entire family was with him. His death was announced by President Jacob Zuma.


Awards and Honors


Nelson Mandela Bridge in Johannesburg Mandela is widely regarded by South Africans as the "Father of the Nation". A statue of Mandela was installed in the Sandton Square shopping center and the center was renamed Nelson Mandela Square. In South Africa, he is often called Madiba, which is a term of respect for elders. In November 2009, the United Nations General Assembly declared his birthday (July 18) as 'Mandela Day' in recognition of his contribution to the anti-apartheid struggle.
