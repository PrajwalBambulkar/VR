Mahatma Jyotiba Phule


Mahatma Jotirao Govindrao Phule (11 April 1827 â€“ 28 November 1890) was an Indian social reformer, social enlightener, thinker, social worker, writer, philosopher and revolutionary activist. He is also known as Mahatma Phule and "Jotiba Phule". In September 1873, he formed an organization named Satya Shodhak Samaj in Maharashtra. He did many works for the upliftment of women and backward and untouchables. He was a strong advocate of providing education to all sections of the society. He was against caste-based division and discrimination prevailing in the Indian society.


Their basic objective has been to provide the right to education to women, oppose child marriage, support widow marriage. Phule wanted to free the society from the evil practice, the trap of superstition. She spent her whole life in providing education to women, in making women aware of their rights. In the 19th century, women were not given education. Phule wanted to save women from gender discrimination. He built India's first school for girls in Pune. Phule was very disturbed and saddened by the pathetic condition of women at that time, that is why he firmly decided that he would continue to bring revolutionary changes in the society. He himself provided education to his wife Savitribai Phule. Savitribai Phule was the first female teacher of India.


1. Early life :

A statue of Jyotiba Phule located in Karad Mahatma Jyotiba Phule was born in Pune in 1827 AD. His mother passed away when he was only one year old. He was brought up by a woman. His family came to Pune from Satara many generations ago and started making Gajras of flowers etc. That's why these people engaged in the work of gardener were known as 'Phule'. Jyotiba studied in Marathi till some time ago, dropped out and later at the age of 21 completed her seventh grade studies in English. He was married in 1840 to Savitri Bai, who later became a famous social worker herself. Both husband and wife worked together in the field of Dalit and women's education. He was a hard worker and a social worker.


2. Workspace :

He did a lot of work for the welfare of widows and women, along with this he also made a lot of efforts to improve the condition of farmers and their welfare. Phule opened a school in 1848 to improve the condition of women and for their education. It was the first school in the country for this work. He could not find a teacher to teach the girls, so he himself made his wife Savitri Phule eligible by doing this work for a few days. Some people tried to obstruct his work from the beginning, but when Phule kept moving forward, he put pressure on his father and got the husband and wife out of the house, because of this his work stopped for some time, but soon he Opened three schools for girls one after the other.

4. Mahatma title :

Jyotiba established 'Satyashodhak Samaj' in 1873 to provide justice to the poor and weak. Seeing his social service, in 1888 he was given the title of 'Mahatma' in a huge meeting in Mumbai. Jyotiba started the marriage rituals without the Brahmin-priest and it was also recognized by the Bombay High Court. He was against child marriage and was a supporter of widow remarriage.
