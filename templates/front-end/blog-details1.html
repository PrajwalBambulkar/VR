Bhagat Singh


Bhagat Singh (Born: 28 September 1907[a] , Martyrdom: 23 March 1931) was a great freedom fighter and revolutionary of India. Along with Chandrashekhar Azad and other party members, he fought the powerful British government with unprecedented courage for India's independence. First the assassination of Bernie Sanders in Lahore and then the bomb blast in Delhi's Central Parliament (Central Assembly) raised open rebellion against the British Empire. He refused to run even after throwing a bomb in the assembly. As a result, the British government hanged him on March 23, 1931, along with his two other companions, Rajguru and Sukhdev.


1. Birth And Environment :

Bhagat Singh's birth is popular on 28 September 1907 (Ashwin Krishna Paksha Saptami) but according to many evidences of the time, he was born in a Sikh family on 27 September 1907. [a] His father's name was Sardar Kishan Singh and mother's name was Vidyavati. Kaur was He was from a farmer[9] family. The Jallianwala Bagh massacre that took place in Amritsar on 13 April 1919 had a deep impact on Bhagat Singh's thinking. Bhagat Singh had founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha for the independence of India after leaving the studies of the National College of Lahore.


After the Chauri Chaura massacre in the year 1922, Bhagat Singh was very disappointed when Gandhiji did not support the farmers. After that his faith in non-violence weakened and he came to the conclusion that armed revolution is the only way to get freedom. After that he became a part of the Gadar Dal formed under the leadership of Chandrashekhar Azad. In the Kakori incident, Bhagat Singh was so disturbed by the hanging of 04 revolutionaries including Ram Prasad 'Bismil' and the imprisonment of 16 others that he along with Chandrashekhar Azad joined his party Hindustan Republican Association and gave it a new name Hindustan Socialist Republican Association. . The aim of this organization was to prepare young men capable of service, sacrifice and suffering.


Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru, had killed British officer J.P. Saunders, who was Assistant Superintendent of Police in Lahore, on 17 December 1928. Revolutionary Chandrashekhar Azad had fully helped him in this action. Along with fellow revolutionary Batukeshwar Dutt, Bhagat Singh threw bombs and pamphlets on 8 April 1929 in the Parliament House, the auditorium of the then Central Assembly of British India, located in present-day New Delhi, to wake up the British government. After throwing the bomb, both of them also gave their arrest there.


2. Revolutionary ActivitiesBhagat Singh :

At that time Bhagat Singh was about twelve years old when the Jallianwala Bagh massacre took place. As soon as he got information about this, Bhagat Singh reached Jallianwala Bagh by walking 12 miles from his school. At this age, Bhagat Singh used to read the revolutionary books of his uncles and wonder whether his path was right or not? After Gandhiji's non-cooperation movement started, he started choosing a path for himself between Gandhiji's non-violent methods and the violent movement of the revolutionaries.


Due to the cancellation of Gandhi ji's non-cooperation movement, some anger arose in him, but like the whole nation, he also respected Mahatma Gandhi. But instead of Gandhiji's non-violent movement, he did not consider it inappropriate to adopt the path of violent revolution for the freedom of the country. He started participating in processions and became a member of many revolutionary parties. The main revolutionaries of his party were Chandrashekhar Azad, Sukhdev, Rajguru etc. In the Kakori incident, Bhagat Singh was so disturbed by the death sentences of 4 revolutionaries and imprisonment of 16 others that he merged his party Naujawan Bharat Sabha with Hindustan Republican Association in 1928 and gave it a new name Hindustan Socialist Republican Association. 10]


3. Vengeance of Lalaji's death :

HSRA pamphlets after Saunders' murder.
In 1928 there were fierce demonstrations to boycott the Simon Commission. The British government also lathi-charged the participants in these demonstrations. Lala Lajpat Rai died after being hurt by this lathi charge. Couldn't live with them anymore. Under a secret plan, he thought of a plan to kill Police Superintendent Scott. According to the thought plan, Bhagat Singh and Rajguru started walking in front of Lahore Kotwali in a busy posture. On the other hand, Jaigopal sat down with his bicycle as if it had broken down. Both became alert on Gopal's signal. On the other hand, Chandrashekhar Azad was working as a guard in carrying out the incident by hiding near the boundary wall of the nearby D.A.L.V. school.


On December 17, 1928, at around 4.15 a.m., as soon as ASP Saunders arrived, Rajguru fired a bullet directly into his head, which would have killed him earlier. But soon after, Bhagat Singh also made complete arrangements for his death by firing 3-4 bullets. As soon as these two were running away, a constable Chanan Singh started chasing them. Chandrashekhar Azad warned him - "If he moves forward, I will shoot him." On not agreeing, Azad shot him and he died on the spot. In this way these people took revenge for the death of Lala Lajpat Rai.


4. Bomb the Assembly :

Although Bhagat Singh was not in favor of bloodshed, but he believed in leftist ideology, and he was related to the principles of Karl Marx and he was promoting the same ideology. However, he was also a staunch supporter of socialism. In the past, his opponents have been accusing him of tricking the youth in the name of Bhagat Singh by telling him their ideology. Despite the Congress being in power, the Congress could not get Bhagat Singh the status of a martyr, because they only used Bhagat Singh's name to connect the youth to their party. He did not like the capitalists' policy of exploitation towards the workers. At that time, since the British were the surveyors and very few Indian industrialists were able to progress, it was natural for them to protest against the atrocities of the British towards the labourers. It was their group's decision not to let the anti-labour policies be passed in the British Parliament. Everyone wanted that the British should know that Indians have woken up and there is anger in their hearts towards such policies. To do this, he had planned to throw a bomb in the Central Assembly of Delhi.


Bhagat Singh wanted it to not have any bloodshed and that his 'voice' could also reach the British. Although in the beginning all the people of his party did not think so, but in the end the names of Bhagat Singh and Batukeshwar Dutt were unanimously chosen. According to the schedule, on 8 April 1929, both of them threw a bomb in the Central Assembly at a place where no one was present, otherwise they could get hurt. The whole hall was filled with smoke.


Bhagat Singh could have run away if he wanted, but he had already thought that he would accept the punishment, even if it was hanging; So he refused to run. At that time both of them were wearing khaki shirt and shorts. After the bomb exploded, they chanted "Inquilab-zindabad, imperialism-murdabad!" [11] raised slogans and threw the pamphlets they had brought with them in the air. Shortly thereafter the police arrived and both were arrested.


5. Jail Days :

First page of Bismil's autobiography published from Sindh. This book was sent to Bhagat Singh in Lahore Jail.
Bhagat Singh remained in jail for almost 2 years. During this, he used to express his revolutionary thoughts by writing articles. His studies continued continuously even while in jail. His articles and letters written during that time to his relatives are still the mirror of his thoughts. In his writings, he has described capitalists as his enemies in many ways. He wrote that the person who exploits the laborers, even if he is an Indian, is their enemy. He also wrote an article in English in jail titled Why am I an atheist? In jail, Bhagat Singh and his companions went on a hunger strike for 64 days. One of his companions, Yatindranath Das, had sacrificed his life in hunger strike.


6. Hanging :

Death Certificate of Bhagat Singh
On August 26, 1930, the court convicted Bhagat Singh under sections 129, 302 of the Indian Penal Code and sections 4 and 6F of the Explosive Substances Act and section 120 of the IPC. On October 7, 1930, the court gave a 68-page verdict, in which Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev and Rajguru were sentenced to death. Section 144 was imposed in Lahore as soon as the death sentence was pronounced. After this, an appeal was filed in the Privy Council for pardon of Bhagat Singh's death sentence, but this appeal was rejected on January 10, 1931. After this, the then Congress President Pt. Madan Mohan Malviya filed an appeal before the Viceroy for pardon on February 14, 1931, that using his privilege, he should waive the death sentence on the basis of humanity. Mahatma Gandhi spoke to the Viceroy on February 17, 1931, to pardon Bhagat Singh's death sentence, and then on February 18, 1931, on behalf of the general public, also filed an appeal with various arguments in front of the Viceroy for pardon. All this was happening against the wishes of Bhagat Singh because Bhagat Singh did not want his sentence to be forgiven.
Bhagat Singh and his two companions Sukhdev and Rajguru were hanged on March 23, 1931 at around 7.33 pm. Before going to the gallows, he was reading Lenin's biography and when he was asked his last wish So he said that he was reading Lenin's biography and that he should be given time to complete it. When the prison authorities informed him that the time had come for his execution, he is said to have said- "Wait! First one revolutionary should meet the other." Then after a minute, the book bounced towards the ceiling and said - "Okay now let's go."

While going to the gallows, all three of them were singing merrily -

When the people of the village saw the fire burning, they came closer. Fearing this, the British threw the half-burnt pieces of his dead body into the Sutlej river and ran away. When the villagers came near, they collected the pieces of his dead body and duly cremated it. And Bhagat Singh became immortal forever. After this, people started considering Gandhi as responsible for his death along with the British. For this reason, when Gandhi was going to attend the Lahore session of Congress, people welcomed Gandhiji with black flags. Gandhi was also attacked at a few places, but the police walking with him in plain uniform saved him.


    7. Persona :

    His thoughts can be gauged from the letters and articles written by him during his jail days. He lamented the distance caused by script (Punjabi's Gurmukhi and Shahmukhi and Hindi and Arabic and Urdu in particular), caste and religion in Indian society. He also considered the attack of an Indian on the weaker section of the society with the same strictness as the atrocities done by an Englishman.


    Apart from Hindi, Urdu, Punjabi and English, Bhagat Singh also knew Bangla which he learned from Batukeshwar Dutt. He believed that his martyrdom would further disturb the Indian public and this could hardly happen if he remained alive. For this reason, he flatly refused to write an apology even after being sentenced to death. Bhagat Singh followed the guidelines given by Pandit Ram Prasad 'Bismil' in his autobiography . He also wrote a letter to the British government, in which it was said that he should be considered a prisoner of war symbolizing the war of Indians against the British government and instead of being hanged, he should be shot.     8. Fame and Honor :

    Memorial of the three martyrs in Hussainiwala The news of his death was published by the Daily Tribune of Lahore and the Daily Worker, a newspaper in New York. Even after this, articles on him were published in many Marxist papers, but since there was a ban on the publication of Marxist papers in India in those days, Indian intellectuals did not know about it. His martyrdom was remembered across the country.


    In South India, Periyar was famous for his article "Why am I an Atheist?" But wrote an editorial in Tamil in the 22-29 March 1931 issue of his weekly paper Kudai Aarasu. In this, Bhagat Singh was praised and his martyrdom was seen as a victory over British imperialism. Even today the people of India and Pakistan see Bhagat Singh as a lover of freedom who devoted his whole life including his youth for the country.His life inspired many Hindi film characters.
